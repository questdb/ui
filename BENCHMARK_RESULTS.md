## Setup

| | |
|---|---|
| Date | 2026-06-12 |
| Grids | legacy `grid.js` (`?useNewGrid=0`) vs React `ResultGrid` (`?useNewGrid=1`) |
| Viewport | 1600 √ó 900 window; grid viewport ‚âà 1510 px wide, ‚âà 340‚Äì420 px tall (~10‚Äì14 visible rows) |
| Data | synthetic, served from a constant canned page at a fixed 10 ms latency (zero network/API variance) |
| Metric | **input ‚Üí fully repainted**: ms from each action to the frame where every visible cell shows data and the rendered cells cover the viewport |

The two grids are driven through the exact same actions and the same synthetic
data. Both run the same per-step settle loop, so the numbers are directly
comparable.

### How to read the numbers

- **median / p95 / min / max** are per-step settle latencies (lower is better).
  Median and p95 are the comparable figures; max captures the worst stall.
- **total** is the summed settle time over all steps. Step counts now match
  between the two grids for every case, so `total` is directly comparable.
- Two effects are baked into the absolute numbers equally for both grids and are
  **not** pure render cost:
  - **Paging debounce + latency.** A scroll/jump into an unloaded region waits a
    ~75 ms load debounce plus the 10 ms mock latency before cells fill. This
    dominates the vertical-scroll and corner-jump medians for *both* grids.
  - **Settle floor.** One animation frame (~8 ms on this machine) is the floor
    for any move that needs no fetch.

that touches a **wide** result ‚Äî horizontal scroll, Home/End, corner jumps ‚Äî
is meaningfully faster on `ResultGrid` thanks to column virtualization, which
also tightens the tail (max) on horizontal stepping.

## Per-case results

### 1. Randomized vertical scroll ‚Äî 1,000,000 rows (20 columns, 100 scrolls)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 100 | 96.9 | 101.2 | 3.3 | 103.5 | 9625.2 |
| `ResultGrid` | 100 | 105.0 | 109.1 | 9.5 | 143.5 | 10467.4 |

Both are dominated by the load debounce + latency on each jump into a new page;
the `min` rows are scrolls that land inside an already-cached page.

### 2. Randomized horizontal scroll ‚Äî 10,000 columns (2,000 rows, 100 scrolls)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 100 | 28.9 | 34.6 | 27.5 | 46.9 | 2954.4 |
| `ResultGrid` | 100 | **16.4** | **19.6** | 15.0 | 99.9 | **1770.7** |

No fetch is involved (every row already holds all columns), so this is pure
horizontal render cost. `ResultGrid` virtualizes columns; legacy re-lays-out a
wider band, ~1.8√ó slower at the median.

### 3. Home / End across 10,000 columns ‚Äî 100 End‚ÜíHome combinations (2,000 rows, 200 presses)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 200 | 49.4 | 58.6 | 47.1 | 89.2 | 10175.9 |
| `ResultGrid` | 200 | **29.7** | **38.3** | 23.3 | 48.7 | **6082.8** |

Each press jumps the focused cell from column 0 to column 9,999 (and back).
`ResultGrid` is ~1.7√ó faster and has a tighter p95/max.

### 4. PageDown √ó100 then PageUp √ó100 ‚Äî 10,000 rows (20 columns, 200 presses)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 200 | 8.3 | 9.2 | 4.8 | 11.7 | **1668.2** |
| `ResultGrid` | 200 | 8.2 | 15.1 | 4.1 | 35.5 | 1822.8 |

100 PageDowns (~1,200 rows) then 100 PageUps, all inside a loaded region apart
from the occasional page-boundary fetch. Medians are tied; legacy has the
tighter tail (p95 9.2 / max 11.7 vs 15.1 / 35.5) ‚Äî `ResultGrid` takes an extra
frame on the page that crosses a fetch boundary.

### 5. Corner jumps ‚Äî bottom-right ‚Üí top-left √ó100 (1,000,000 rows √ó 10,000 columns, 200 presses)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 200 | 149.5 | 162.0 | 145.4 | 168.3 | 30192.6 |
| `ResultGrid` | 200 | **118.1** | **125.8** | 114.4 | 139.5 | **23879.2** |

The heaviest case: every jump moves both axes at once (tail-row fetch + render
of a 10,000-column-wide region). `ResultGrid` is ~1.3√ó faster and a touch more
consistent (tighter p95/max).

> Shortcuts used: `ResultGrid` reaches each corner with a single chord
> (`Ctrl+Home` / `Ctrl+End`). The legacy grid has no single-chord corner jump, so
> the harness sends its equivalent sequence ‚Äî a column key then a `Cmd`+arrow row
> key (`Home`‚Üí`Cmd+‚Üë`, `End`‚Üí`Cmd+‚Üì`) ‚Äî timed as one move.

## Caveats

- **Small viewport.** The result pane is ~340‚Äì420 px tall (~10‚Äì14 visible rows).
  A taller pane renders more cells per frame and would widen the wide-result
  deltas (cases 2/3/5).
- **Debounce-bound cases.** Vertical-scroll and corner medians are mostly the
  75 ms load debounce + 10 ms mock latency, not paint. The interesting render
  signal there is the spread (`min`/`max`), not the median.
- **Virtual-row mapping differs.** For 1,000,000 rows, `ResultGrid` caps the
  scroll canvas (`MAX_VIRTUAL_ROWS` ‚âà 333k) and maps head 1:1 with a tail leap;
  legacy scales proportionally. Random vertical scroll therefore visits slightly
  different absolute rows on each grid, but the per-frame render cost is the same
  kind of work.
- **Synthetic, constant page.** Every page serves the same canned rows, so cell
  formatting cost is realistic but row-to-row variety is not. This isolates
  render/scroll cost, which is the point.
