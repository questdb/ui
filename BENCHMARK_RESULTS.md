# Result grid benchmark results — legacy `grid.js` vs `ResultGrid`

Generated by the harness in [BENCHMARK.md](BENCHMARK.md) using
[`e2e/benchmark/gridBench.js`](e2e/benchmark/gridBench.js) and the
`mock.pagination` data source ([`src/scenes/Result/benchmarkMock.ts`](src/scenes/Result/benchmarkMock.ts)).

## Setup

| | |
|---|---|
| Date | 2026-06-12 |
| Grids | legacy `grid.js` (`?useNewGrid=0`) vs React `ResultGrid` (`?useNewGrid=1`) |
| Viewport | 1600 × 900 window; grid viewport ≈ 1510 px wide, ≈ 340–420 px tall (~10–14 visible rows) |
| Data | synthetic, served from a constant canned page at a fixed 10 ms latency (zero network/API variance) |
| Metric | **input → fully repainted**: ms from each action to the frame where every visible cell shows data and the rendered cells cover the viewport |

The two grids are driven through the exact same actions and the same synthetic
data. Both run the same per-step settle loop, so the numbers are directly
comparable.

### How to read the numbers

- **median / p95 / min / max** are per-step settle latencies (lower is better).
  Median and p95 are the comparable figures; max captures the worst stall.
- **total** is the summed settle time over all steps — comparable only when the
  step count matches (it differs for PageDown/PageUp, see below).
- Two effects are baked into the absolute numbers equally for both grids and are
  **not** pure render cost:
  - **Paging debounce + latency.** A scroll/jump into an unloaded region waits a
    ~75 ms load debounce plus the 10 ms mock latency before cells fill. This
    dominates the vertical-scroll and corner-jump medians for *both* grids.
  - **Settle floor.** One animation frame (~8 ms on this machine) is the floor
    for any move that needs no fetch.

## Summary (median ms, lower is better)

| Case | Legacy | ResultGrid | Winner |
|---|---:|---:|:--|
| Randomized vertical scroll — 1,000,000 rows | 96.9 | 105.0 | ≈ even (legacy +8%) |
| Randomized horizontal scroll — 10,000 columns | 28.9 | **16.4** | **ResultGrid 1.8×** |
| Home / End across 10,000 columns | 47.6 | **24.8** | **ResultGrid 1.9×** |
| PageDown / PageUp through 10,000 rows | 8.3 | 8.3 | ≈ even |
| Corner jumps — top-left / bottom-right (1,000,000 × 10,000) | 152.8 | **121.3** | **ResultGrid 1.3×** |
| Right arrow through 1,000 columns | 8.3 | 8.3 | ≈ even (lower tail on ResultGrid) |
| Down arrow through 1,000 rows | 8.3 | 8.3 | ≈ even |

**Takeaway:** vertical paging is debounce-bound and effectively tied. Everything
that touches a **wide** result — horizontal scroll, Home/End, corner jumps —
is meaningfully faster on `ResultGrid` thanks to column virtualization, which
also tightens the tail (max) on horizontal stepping.

## Per-case results

### 1. Randomized vertical scroll — 1,000,000 rows (20 columns, 100 scrolls)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 100 | 96.9 | 101.2 | 3.3 | 103.5 | 9625.2 |
| `ResultGrid` | 100 | 105.0 | 109.1 | 9.5 | 143.5 | 10467.4 |

Both are dominated by the load debounce + latency on each jump into a new page;
the `min` rows are scrolls that land inside an already-cached page.

### 2. Randomized horizontal scroll — 10,000 columns (2,000 rows, 100 scrolls)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 100 | 28.9 | 34.6 | 27.5 | 46.9 | 2954.4 |
| `ResultGrid` | 100 | **16.4** | **19.6** | 15.0 | 99.9 | **1770.7** |

No fetch is involved (every row already holds all columns), so this is pure
horizontal render cost. `ResultGrid` virtualizes columns; legacy re-lays-out a
wider band, ~1.8× slower at the median.

### 3. Home / End across 10,000 columns (2,000 rows, 60 alternations)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 60 | 47.6 | 58.6 | 45.6 | 79.4 | 2980.6 |
| `ResultGrid` | 60 | **24.8** | **30.5** | 19.9 | 34.5 | **1525.5** |

Each press jumps the focused cell from column 0 to column 9,999 (and back).
`ResultGrid` is ~1.9× faster and has a much tighter p95/max.

### 4. PageDown / PageUp through 10,000 rows (20 columns)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 2000 | 8.3 | 9.4 | 3.0 | 90.5 | 16779.5 |
| `ResultGrid` | 1668 | 8.3 | 10.9 | 5.7 | 281.3 | 14647.1 |

Median is identical (most PageDowns stay inside a loaded page → one frame). Step
counts differ because the two grids' result panes have slightly different
heights, so rows-per-page differs; compare median/p95, not total. The `max` is a
page-boundary fetch on each grid.

### 5. Corner jumps — top-left / bottom-right (1,000,000 rows × 10,000 columns, 40 alternations)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 40 | 152.8 | 164.1 | 149.6 | 170.2 | 6157.7 |
| `ResultGrid` | 40 | **121.3** | **125.9** | 118.5 | 126.6 | **4877.4** |

The heaviest case: every jump moves both axes at once (tail-row fetch + render
of a 10,000-column-wide region). `ResultGrid` is ~1.3× faster and far more
consistent (p95/max within a few ms of the median, vs a wider spread on legacy).

> Shortcuts used: `ResultGrid` reaches each corner with a single chord
> (`Ctrl+Home` / `Ctrl+End`). The legacy grid has no single-chord corner jump, so
> the harness sends its equivalent sequence — a column key then a `Cmd`+arrow row
> key (`Home`→`Cmd+↑`, `End`→`Cmd+↓`) — timed as one move.

### 6. Right arrow through 1,000 columns (2,000 rows, 999 presses)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 999 | 8.3 | 11.0 | 3.8 | 84.5 | 8407.3 |
| `ResultGrid` | 999 | 8.3 | **9.5** | 5.0 | **18.2** | 8326.1 |

Even at the median (most steps move within the rendered band). `ResultGrid` has
the lower tail — legacy spikes to 84 ms when an arrow scrolls a fresh column
band into view, `ResultGrid` stays ≤ 18 ms.

### 7. Down arrow through 1,000 rows (20 columns, 999 presses)

| Grid | steps | median | p95 | min | max | total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| legacy `grid.js` | 999 | 8.3 | 9.2 | 3.1 | 14.8 | 8311.4 |
| `ResultGrid` | 999 | 8.3 | 9.3 | 4.4 | 12.6 | 8323.2 |

Single-row vertical stepping is one frame on both grids — effectively identical.

## Caveats

- **Small viewport.** The result pane is ~340–420 px tall (~10–14 visible rows).
  A taller pane renders more cells per frame and would widen the wide-result
  deltas (cases 2/3/5).
- **Debounce-bound cases.** Vertical-scroll and corner medians are mostly the
  75 ms load debounce + 10 ms mock latency, not paint. The interesting render
  signal there is the spread (`min`/`max`), not the median.
- **Virtual-row mapping differs.** For 1,000,000 rows, `ResultGrid` caps the
  scroll canvas (`MAX_VIRTUAL_ROWS` ≈ 333k) and maps head 1:1 with a tail leap;
  legacy scales proportionally. Random vertical scroll therefore visits slightly
  different absolute rows on each grid, but the per-frame render cost is the same
  kind of work.
- **Synthetic, constant page.** Every page serves the same canned rows, so cell
  formatting cost is realistic but row-to-row variety is not. This isolates
  render/scroll cost, which is the point.
